Reviewing the Original Bitcoin Paper: A First Pass

30 April 2020

We are taking a 3 pass approach as outlined in Keshav's Paper

With this approach, you go through paper in three gradual passes, each unfolding the complexity of the paper progressively. Keeping it in separate passes allows you to assess the content in different resolutions: form a gestalt and then progressively refine in on the details.

Using this approach, we filtered Satoshi Nakomoto's original paper and we produce our assessment here. It is accompanied with answers to the five questions that the first pass is required to produce.

Even on a preliminary read it is clear that the author has concentrated not on the correctness part of engineering or to show a theoretical proof, but was fully oriented towards creating a foundation for a practical running system. The most interesting part in the paper’s abstract highlights the need for creating a body of work that acts as a long term source of truth. The place where the largest amount of work has been put forth surely seems like a clever engineering technique at the user experience level. The leverage of human factors for engineering the system can be sensed when he lays down observations like the possibility of largest CPU power or the nodes in charge doesn't actively go against the system, as in an auto immune disease. It is clear that the author has looked at this idea from multiple perspectives and has come up with a somewhat holistic approach to creating a digital currency. It is a commendable effort put forth from the author in this respect.
