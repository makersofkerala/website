Learn to Build Compilers

We are building a WebAssembly compiler for a project we initiated 2 years ago called Thengascript, a way to program web applications in Malayalam, our mother tongue.

Towards this purpose, we began looking for resources to help us to understand the craft of compiler engineering. This is a round-up of promising resources we collected. We have catalogued them in a way to help a beginner interested in making compilers, to proceed step by step, to become an expert. If you think we have left out any great resources, please reach out to us.

Compilers are curious creatures. They take in a language, roughly comprehensible by humans, as its input and turns it into a form comprehensible by machines. This translation can happen in a series of steps, each of which can produce an intermediate language with a certain set of good properties. After these whole series of translations is done, something magical happens in the end: it makes human-computer interaction possible. If peeking under their hood to understand how things work spark joy when you find out, compiler construction could be right up your alley.

First of all, we present a roadmap we have used to understand compilers. The selection here is chosen in a way it unfolds their complexity, a few steps at a time. This is done under the light of our own exploration of literature and media on compilers. We curate the easily accessible ones that form a rough progression in their complexity as a list. We have also tried to give credit to the people who have made this as they are the real heroes who have dedicated their time and effort to lend their broad shoulders for building novel things on top of them. This roadmap is followed by a collection of materials, we have compiled by keeping a lookout for relevant materials over the past couple of years.
